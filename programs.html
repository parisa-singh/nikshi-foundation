Program 01

Transgender
Empowerment

The transgender community in India faces systemic exclusion from employment, education, and social services. At Nikshi Foundation, we believe the most powerful form of support is creating sustainable independence — not charity, but capability.

We establish small enterprises run by transgender individuals, provide vocational skills training, and actively connect community members with employers across Bangalore.

✓
Business Setup Support
Establishing small enterprises and providing the initial resources and guidance to run them.
✓
Vocational Training
Skills workshops in areas like tailoring, food service, and digital literacy.
✓
Employment Placement
Facilitating connections with inclusive employers across Bangalore's organisations.

Program 02

Fight Against
Hunger

Food insecurity is one of the most immediate and solvable crises in urban India. Every day, thousands of people in Bangalore go hungry — and every day, we show up with freshly cooked meals and ration kits to change that.

Our hunger relief work runs on three tracks: daily prepared meals, monthly ration kit distribution, and targeted COVID-19 relief in rural and urban pockets of Karnataka.

Program 03

Education for
Every Child

Thousands of children in Bangalore's slums and informal settlements have no access to formal schooling. Without basic literacy and numeracy, the cycle of poverty continues. We work to break that cycle.

Our education initiative brings classes directly into communities — teaching reading, writing, and arithmetic — and provides every child with the books and supplies they need to learn.

✓
Foundational Learning
Reading, writing, and basic numeracy for children aged 5–14 who cannot attend school.
✓
Books & Supplies Distribution
Textbooks, notebooks, pencils, and stationery distributed at no cost to families.
✓
Community Classrooms
Learning sessions held in community spaces — temples, open grounds, and local halls.
